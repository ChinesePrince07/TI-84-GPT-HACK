What is a Transistor?

A transistor is an electrically controlled switch

A small signal controls a larger current — no moving parts

Mechanical Switch

  • Physical contact opens/closes
  • Slow (milliseconds)
  • Wears out over time
  • Large, bulky

Transistor Switch

  • Electric field controls current
  • Fast (nanoseconds)
  • No wear, solid state
  • Nanometer scale
2

Two Main Types

BJT

Bipolar Junction Transistor

  • Current-controlled
  • Base current controls collector current
  • $I_C = \beta \cdot I_B$
  • Higher power consumption
  • Used in: amplifiers, analog

MOSFET

Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor FET

  • Voltage-controlled
  • Gate voltage controls drain current
  • $V_{GS} > V_{th} \Rightarrow$ ON
  • Extremely low power
  • Used in: CPUs, digital circuits

99%+ of transistors made today are MOSFETs

3

Why MOSFETs Dominate

The Key Advantage: Gate Draws No DC Current

BJT:

  • Base needs continuous current
  • Power = $I_B \cdot V_{BE}$ always
  • Heat builds up at scale

MOSFET:

  • Gate is insulated (SiO₂)
  • No DC current into gate
  • Only power during switching
$$P_{static} \approx 0 \text{ (ideal MOSFET)}$$

This is why your phone has 10 billion transistors and doesn't melt

AP Problem: The Circuit

Circuit Components:

  • $V_{DD} = 5.0$ V supply
  • $R_L = 1.0$ kΩ load resistor
  • $R_G = 10$ kΩ gate resistor
  • $C_G = 50$ pF gate capacitance
  • MOSFET: $V_{th} = 1.5$ V, $R_{DS} = 5\Omega$
  • Gate oxide: $t_{ox} = 50$ nm

When gate is LOW: MOSFET OFF, no current

When gate is HIGH: MOSFET ON, current flows through $R_L$

AP Free Response Problem

Transistor Switching Circuit Analysis

Using the circuit shown, where the MOSFET acts as a switch (ON: $R_{DS} = 5\Omega$, OFF: open):

(a) When ON, calculate the current through $R_L$ and the power dissipated in the load. (3 pts)

(b) Calculate the time constant for charging the gate capacitor $C_G$ through $R_G$. (2 pts)

(c) Starting from 0V, how long after applying 5V to the input does the MOSFET turn ON? (3 pts)

(d) Calculate the electric field magnitude in the gate oxide when $V_{GS} = 5.0$ V. (2 pts)

(e) If the MOSFET switches at 1 MHz and the gate fully charges each cycle, find the average power consumed charging the gate capacitor. (3 pts)

6

Solution (a) & (b)

(a) Current and Power when ON: [3 pts]

When ON, total resistance = $R_{DS} + R_L = 5 + 1000 = 1005\Omega$

$$I = \frac{V_{DD}}{R_{DS} + R_L} = \frac{5.0\text{ V}}{1005\text{ }\Omega} = \boxed{4.98\text{ mA}}$$

Power in load: $P_L = I^2 R_L = (4.98\times10^{-3})^2 \times 1000 = \boxed{24.8\text{ mW}}$

(b) Gate RC Time Constant: [2 pts]

$$\tau = R_G C_G = (10 \times 10^3)(50 \times 10^{-12}) = \boxed{500\text{ ns}}$$
7

Solution (c) & (d)

(c) Time to Turn ON: [3 pts]

Gate charges: $V_G(t) = V_{DD}(1 - e^{-t/\tau})$. Turns ON when $V_G = V_{th} = 1.5$ V:

$$1.5 = 5.0(1 - e^{-t/\tau}) \implies e^{-t/\tau} = 0.7$$
$$t = -\tau \ln(0.7) = 500\text{ ns} \times 0.357 = \boxed{178\text{ ns}}$$

(d) Electric Field in Gate Oxide: [2 pts]

$$E = \frac{V_{GS}}{t_{ox}} = \frac{5.0\text{ V}}{50 \times 10^{-9}\text{ m}} = \boxed{1.0 \times 10^8\text{ V/m}}$$
8

Solution (e)

(e) Dynamic Power Consumption: [3 pts]

Energy to charge capacitor to voltage V:

$$E = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{1}{2}(50 \times 10^{-12})(5.0)^2 = 6.25 \times 10^{-10}\text{ J}$$

At $f = 1$ MHz:

$$P = E \cdot f = (6.25 \times 10^{-10})(10^6) = \boxed{0.625\text{ mW}}$$

Key insight: Power scales with $f$ and $V^2$ — this is why chips use lower voltages!

MOSFET Structure

Gate voltage → Electric field through oxide → Attracts electrons → Channel forms → Current flows

How MOSFETs Work

The Physics:

  1. Apply $V_{GS}$ to gate
  2. E-field: $E = V_{GS}/t_{ox}$
  3. Field repels holes from surface
  4. Field attracts electrons
  5. Electron layer = channel
  6. Current flows D → S

OFF: $V_{GS} < V_{th}$

No channel, no current

ON: $V_{GS} > V_{th}$

Channel forms, current flows

$$V_{GS} > V_{th} \Rightarrow \text{ON}$$

Why Are Resistors Needed?

Pull-Up Resistors (1kΩ):

  • Define HIGH state — pull line to 3.3V when idle
  • Limit current — when line pulled LOW: $I = V/R = 3.3\text{mA}$
  • Set speed — RC time constant with wire capacitance

Without pull-ups: Lines would float at undefined voltage — random noise!

Why 1kΩ specifically?

  • Too low: Wastes power, heats up
  • Too high: Slow rise time, noise pickup
  • 1kΩ: Good balance — fast enough for data, low power
$$\tau = RC \approx 1\text{k}\Omega \times 20\text{pF} = 20\text{ns}$$
14

MOSFET Level Shifter

How It Works:

Idle (both HIGH):

$V_{GS} = 3.3 - 3.3 = 0V < V_{th}$

MOSFET OFF — sides isolated


One side LOW:

$V_{GS} = 3.3 - 0 = 3.3V > V_{th}$

MOSFET ON — pulls other side LOW

$$V_{GS} = V_G - V_S$$
15

Why the MOSFET is Crucial

Without MOSFET:

  • Direct wire → signals can't be isolated
  • Can't do bidirectional properly
  • No automatic level translation

With MOSFET:

  • Auto-switching — $V_{GS}$ changes with signal
  • Bidirectional — works both ways
  • Isolation — sides independent when idle
  • Low power — no gate current

The MOSFET's field-effect switching makes this possible
